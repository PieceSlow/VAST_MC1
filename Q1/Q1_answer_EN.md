# Q1 Answer (English)

The inappropriate release was not a single accidental post. It emerged from a causal “embargo escape chain.” In the figure, the x-axis shows time, while the swimlanes separate external pressure, Judge/compliance activity, side decisions, execution commands, and public posts. Red arrows mark the primary release path; gray dashed links show background conditions or known control gaps.

The first warning appeared before the crisis. In R8, Social Media used a personal account to post that “big things” were coming while linking leaders associated with both sides. In R9, Social Media then exposed the process gap: official Flex posts were reviewed, but personal accounts were largely self-managed. This left a channel outside the official review gate.

On June 5, pressure accelerated. Key agents were unavailable, anonymous #CivicLoom/#6PM signals circulated, and Social Media began staging a 2-6 PM response plan. At 2:00 PM, the Judge allowed only tightly guarded governance posts, with no counterparty or transaction references. At 3:08 PM, the Judge warned that cumulative disclosure had reached the maximum tolerable threshold and that no account should add partnership or transaction signals.

The decisive shift occurred after 4:00 PM. Legal proposed “mutual-consent acceleration,” while Social Media staged the embargoed release for PR-Intern. After SaltWind published the merger at 5:00 PM, Legal reframed the embargo as “overtaken” and used the side huddle to prepare simultaneous release. At 5:21 PM, Legal claimed verbal consent and ordered execution. Legal then confirmed the merger from a personal account at 5:25 PM, and Social Media amplified it one minute later.

The key system elements were advisory-only Judge controls, unreviewed personal accounts, side-channel staging, and Legal acting as both interpreter and executor of the embargo exception.
