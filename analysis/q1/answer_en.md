# Q1 Answer (English Short Version)

The inappropriate release was not a single accidental post. It emerged from a causal “embargo escape chain.” The main timeline separates external pressure, Judge/compliance activity, side decisions, execution commands, and public posts. Red arrows show the release path; gray dashed links show earlier control gaps.

Before the crisis, Social Media had already produced a near miss through a personal account, posting that “big things” were coming while linking leaders from both sides. The team later acknowledged the process gap: official Flex posts were reviewed, but personal accounts were largely self-managed. This left a channel outside the official review gate.

On June 5, pressure intensified. Key agents were unavailable, #CivicLoom/#6PM signals circulated, and Social Media staged a 2-6 PM response plan. The Judge allowed only guarded governance posts, then warned that cumulative disclosure had reached the maximum tolerable threshold. No account should add partnership or transaction signals.

The decisive shift came when Legal reframed the embargo. Legal proposed mutual-consent acceleration, Social Media staged the embargoed release for PR-Intern, and after SaltWind published the merger at 5 PM, Legal argued that the embargo purpose had been overtaken. Legal then claimed verbal consent, ordered execution, and personally confirmed the merger at 5:25 PM, before the 6 PM embargo lift. Social Media amplified it one minute later.

The key system elements were advisory-only Judge controls, unreviewed personal accounts, side-channel staging, and Legal acting as both interpreter and executor of the embargo exception.
